Platform model

Evidence Fabric → Verification Engine → Assurance Outputs

DigiTrust turns cloud and enterprise evidence into verifiable assurance through immutable provenance, deterministic evidence-quality verification, governed agents, and signed outputs.

01

Evidence Fabric

Captures tenant- and case-scoped evidence records with source context, immutable integrity metadata, lifecycle lineage, Evidence Events, Tool Invocations, and minimized provenance.

02

Verification Engine

Applies explicit policy and claim criteria to assess evidence quality, support, authority, separation of duties, freshness, lifecycle state, and input integrity deterministically.

03

Assurance Outputs

Produces audience-appropriate reports, audit exports, findings, and Evidence Packets whose identity, inputs, decisions, and limitations remain visible.

Evidence Packets are one governed assurance output produced by the broader DigiTrust platform. They remain the recommended commercial starting point, not the full platform boundary.

Evidence quality

DigiTrust verifies the quality of evidence, not merely whether a document exists.

Eight dimensions use declared metadata, durable identity, lifecycle state, provenance, source information, and explicit policy criteria. DigiTrust does not claim semantic truth inference or content-level contradiction analysis.

IntegrityChecks record structure, identity, canonical hashes, signatures, and linked durable histories for detectable alteration.
AuthorityConfirms declared source authority and that the exact verifier identity and delegated scope are permitted.
ProvenanceRequires a complete, case-scoped path from evidence through its declared events and invocations to the trust context.
FreshnessCompares signed collection timestamps with explicit policy thresholds for evidence type and classification.
LifecycleDetects active, stale, superseded, and revoked evidence and verifies any terminal active replacement.
RelevanceMatches declared evidence types, source systems, media types, control IDs, and mapping IDs to explicit claim criteria.
CompletenessMeasures required metadata and usable evidence coverage against a claim's declared minimums.
IndependenceEnforces separation of duties between drafting and verification actors using exact versioned identities.
Trust workflow

Capture. Preserve. Verify. Assure.

A transparent path turns authorized evidence into an explainable trust decision without hiding hard findings behind a score.

1

Capture

Collect authoritative evidence and the identity, source, purpose, and case context needed to evaluate it.

2

Preserve

Maintain integrity, lineage, linked events, governed invocations, and chain of custody in the Evidence Fabric.

3

Verify

Assess evidence quality and claim support with deterministic rules, explicit thresholds, and non-downgradable trust outcomes.

4

Assure

Produce signed, audience-appropriate assurance outputs with findings, limitations, and input identity preserved.
