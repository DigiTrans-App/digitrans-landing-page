Platform model

Evidence Fabric → Verification Engine → Human Control Plane → Assurance & Audit

DigiTrust separates probabilistic AI execution from the deterministic evidence, authority, verification, approval, authorization, and audit controls required for consequential enterprise use.

01

Evidence Fabric

Captures tenant- and case-scoped records with source context, append-only Evidence Events, governed Tool Invocations, artifact integrity, lifecycle lineage, and minimized provenance.

02

Verification Engine

Applies explicit policy and claim criteria to assess eight evidence-quality dimensions, support, authority, separation of duties, contradictions, disclosure risk, and signed input integrity.

03

Human Control Plane

Binds accountable approval to the exact verified subject, preserves failed history through remediation, and blocks self-approval, authority drift, expiration, revocation, and target substitution.

04

Assurance & Audit

Produces customer-safe outputs and signed audit exports whose identity, evidence, verification, decisions, limitations, and current authorization state remain independently reproducible.

Evidence quality and structured trust

DigiTrust verifies the quality, authority, and permitted use of evidence.

Eight quality dimensions use declared metadata, durable identity, lifecycle state, provenance, source information, and explicit policy criteria. DigiTrust does not infer truth from free-form prose: it compares typed, hash-bound assertions under explicit scope, time, authority, lifecycle, and classification rules.

IntegrityChecks record structure, identity, canonical hashes, signatures, and linked durable histories for detectable alteration.
AuthorityConfirms declared source authority and that the exact verifier identity and delegated scope are permitted.
ProvenanceRequires a complete, case-scoped path from evidence through its declared events and invocations to the trust context.
FreshnessCompares signed collection timestamps with explicit policy thresholds for evidence type and classification.
LifecycleDetects active, stale, superseded, and revoked evidence and verifies any terminal active replacement.
RelevanceMatches declared evidence types, source systems, media types, control IDs, and mapping IDs to explicit claim criteria.
CompletenessMeasures required metadata and usable evidence coverage against a claim's declared minimums.
IndependenceEnforces separation of duties between drafting and verification actors using exact versioned identities.

Contradiction and disclosure controls are explicit and fail closed. Critical unresolved structured contradictions remain hard failures. Secrets, credentials, private keys, system prompts, and cross-tenant content cannot be downgraded by redaction metadata.

Trust lifecycle

Capture. Preserve. Verify. Resolve. Approve. Authorize. Reproduce.

A transparent, deterministic path turns authorized evidence into accountable enterprise use without hiding hard findings behind a score or allowing an agent to become the system of record.

01CaptureRecord authoritative evidence, actors, tools, purpose, classification, and case context.
02PreserveBind provenance, lifecycle, hashes, events, invocations, and append-only chain of custody.
03VerifyApply deterministic quality, claim-support, contradiction, disclosure, and authority rules.
04ResolvePreserve failed findings and require a different later independently verified result.
05ApproveBind an attributable human decision to the exact verified subject and granted conditions.
06AuthorizePermit only the approved subject, version, scope, conditions, authority, and time window.
07ReproduceIndependently verify the complete evidence, decision, remediation, and authorization history.
Enterprise human-control plane

AI can perform the work. Accountable people still control consequential use.

DigiTrust separates generation, verification, and approval. Approval cannot be created by an agent, inferred from free-form text, transferred to another output, or used after expiration or revocation.

01

Exact approval

A human decision applies only to the exact tenant, case, subject, version, verification result, scope, conditions, authority, and time window that were reviewed.

02

Durable remediation

Failed verification remains visible. Correction creates new same-scope evidence and a later independently verified run rather than rewriting the original failure.

03

Replay-safe authorization

Changed content, substituted evidence, authority drift, expired approval, revoked approval, or a mismatched verification report fails closed at the exact-use boundary.

Agents cannot approve their own consequential output. Requestors, bound drafters, verifiers, unauthorized people, and non-human actors are rejected by the durable approval boundary.
